Projects

Five ERP modules, end-to-end

Each project represents a complete business analysis cycle — from problem discovery to implementation design and measurable business impact.

01
Procurement & Dynamic Approval Purchase
Purchase Request → Multi-level Approval → Purchase Order → Receipt + Inventory
Problem

Procurement approvals were manual and inconsistent — different departments followed different processes. Purchase orders were created without proper budget validation or manager sign-off, leading to unauthorized spending and inventory discrepancies.

Solution

Designed a dynamic multi-level approval matrix in Odoo Purchase module. Approval routing adapts based on purchase amount threshold and department. Integrated inventory receipts with cost auto-update on confirmation.

Key Features
  • PR → PO flow with status tracking
  • Amount-based approval routing (L1/L2/L3)
  • Auto inventory update on PO confirmation
  • Landed cost allocation logic
  • Rejection with comment trail
Business Impact
Reduced unauthorized procurement by enforcing approval gates before budget commitment. Eliminated manual handoffs between requestors, approvers, and the warehouse — the entire PR-to-receipt flow became a single traceable chain in Odoo with full audit log.
02
Omnichannel Sales & POS Sales + Retail
Online Order + POS Sale → Unified Stock → Revenue Report
Problem

A retailer operated both a webshop and physical POS but had no unified view of inventory or revenue. Returns processed at POS had inconsistent pricing versus original sale price, causing financial reconciliation errors.

Solution

Integrated Odoo Sales, eCommerce, and Point of Sale modules into a single inventory source of truth. Designed return logic that automatically references original invoice price to eliminate price discrepancy on refunds.

Key Features
  • Shared stock across channels (real-time)
  • Return flow with original price lock
  • Cross-channel revenue dashboard
  • Loyalty program unified logic
  • Automated end-of-day reconciliation
Business Impact
Eliminated stock discrepancy between channels that previously required weekly manual reconciliation. Return accuracy improved significantly — refund amounts now always match original transaction price regardless of channel, removing a major source of accounting disputes.
03
B2B Contract Management B2B / Sales
Master Contract → Sub-Orders → Delivery Tracking → Milestone Invoice
Problem

Enterprise clients negotiated annual master contracts with multiple delivery releases over the year. There was no system to track how much of a contract had been delivered vs. remaining, and invoicing was manual and error-prone.

Solution

Designed a master order + sub-order architecture in Odoo Sales. The master contract holds committed volumes and pricing; sub-orders pull from the contract. Delivered vs. remaining quantities update automatically, and invoices trigger on delivery confirmation.

Key Features
  • Master contract → sub-order linkage
  • Delivered/remaining quantity widget
  • Delivery-based invoice trigger
  • Contract utilization dashboard
  • Price lock from master agreement
Business Impact
Gave sales and finance teams a real-time view of contract fulfillment for the first time. Eliminated over-invoicing risk by tying invoice generation directly to confirmed deliveries. Account managers could now proactively alert clients when approaching contract limits.
04
Education CRM — Admissions CRM / Custom
Parent Lead → Student Profile → Sibling Logic → Enrollment
Problem

An education center needed a custom CRM to manage admissions. The key challenge: a parent is the customer (payer) but each child is the enrolled student, requiring complex parent-child data relationships and dynamic forms based on education level.

Solution

Used Odoo's Delegation Inheritance (_inherits) to build Student as an extension of res.partner, enabling the student record to inherit parent contact data. Dynamic UI fields rendered conditionally based on education level selection. Sibling discount rule applied automatically.

Key Features
  • Parent → child delegation inheritance
  • Dynamic UI by education level (Nursery/Primary/Secondary)
  • Sibling discount auto-calculation
  • Admissions pipeline in Kanban view
  • Academic term enrollment tracking
Business Impact
This project demonstrated advanced Odoo architectural knowledge — using _inherits correctly to avoid data duplication while maintaining a clean relational structure. The sibling discount automation removed a manual calculation step that admissions staff had to do per-family, significantly reducing enrollment processing time.
05
Inventory Transfer — Transit Hub Inventory / WH
Warehouse A → Transit Hub → ASN Email → Warehouse B
Problem

A company with multiple warehouses needed goods to pass through a central transit hub before reaching the destination warehouse. In-transit stock was invisible in the system, and discrepancies at receiving (damaged or missing items) had no handling process.

Solution

Configured Odoo multi-step routing with Transit Location as a virtual intermediate warehouse. ASN (Advance Shipping Notice) emails auto-trigger when goods leave Warehouse A. Discrepancy workflow allows partial receipt with backorder creation and variance logging.

Key Features
  • A → Transit → B routing configuration
  • Automated ASN email on dispatch
  • In-transit stock visibility in real-time
  • Discrepancy handling with backorder
  • Transfer reconciliation report
Business Impact
For the first time, the logistics team had live visibility of goods in transit between warehouses — eliminating the "black hole" period where stock was shipped but not yet received. The ASN automation reduced receiving preparation time at Warehouse B. Discrepancy handling ensured no variance went unrecorded, improving inventory accuracy across the network.
How I Think

ERP Analyst Mindset

Good ERP analysis is not about features — it's about understanding why a business works the way it does, and designing a system that makes the right behavior easy.

The Fit/Gap Approach

Before recommending any customization, I always exhaust the configuration options first. Customization has a long-term cost — maintenance, upgrades, developer dependency. The right approach is a disciplined ladder:

01
Standard Feature
Does Odoo already do this out-of-the-box?
02
Configuration
Can settings, security rules, or workflows cover the gap?
03
Process Workaround
Can the business adapt its process to fit the system?
04
Custom Development
Only when the above three cannot satisfy the requirement.
ERP as a Single Source of Truth

The most important ERP principle: every piece of business data should live in exactly one place. Disconnected spreadsheets, duplicate databases, and manual reconciliation are symptoms of a system that isn't trusted.

Ask "where does this data currently live?" before modeling any requirement
Map every integration point — what feeds into the module, what does it feed out
Design for the exception, not just the happy path — what happens when it fails?
Validate with real users using process walkthroughs, not just UAT scripts
Document the AS-IS before proposing TO-BE — understand before redesigning
Measure business impact in concrete terms: time saved, errors eliminated, steps reduced
